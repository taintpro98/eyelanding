# Trading Signals

The Signals page is the heart of ALumiEye. It shows every buy and sell signal detected by the AI engine in real time, across all assets in your subscribed market.

---

## What Is a Signal?

A signal is a notification that the AI has detected a potential trading opportunity. It does **not** tell you to blindly buy or sell — it tells you that certain conditions are met that have historically preceded a price move.

Think of it like a weather forecast: the AI says "conditions look favourable for a move upward." You still decide whether to act.

---

## Reading the Signals Table

Each row in the signals table contains:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Symbol** | The asset ticker (e.g. `VNM`, `BTCUSDT`) |
| **Type** | `BUY` (green badge) or `SELL` (red badge) |
| **Quantity** | Signal strength expressed as a percentage |
| **Price** | The asset's price at the moment the signal was generated |
| **Time** | How long ago the signal was created (e.g. "5 minutes ago") |

### Signal Types

Beyond just buy and sell, each signal has a sub-type that describes what the AI detected:

| Signal Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | A new position opportunity is detected — first signal for a move |
| **Increase** | An existing buy signal is getting stronger — consider adding to a position |
| **Decrease** | An existing buy signal is weakening — consider reducing exposure |
| **Close** | The move appears to be ending — consider closing a position |

---

## Understanding Signal Strength (Quantity)

The **Quantity** column shows how strong the signal is, expressed as a percentage. The higher the number, the more confident the AI is.

| Strength | Colour | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **20% and above** | Red | Very strong — the AI has high conviction |
| **10% – 20%** | Orange | Strong — worth close attention |
| **2% – 10%** | Blue | Moderate — valid but less urgent |
| **Below 2%** | Grey | Weak — treat with caution |

> **Beginner tip:** If you are new, focus on signals at **10% or above**. Stronger signals have a higher historical hit rate. Do not act on every signal you see — wait for the ones that align with your own view.

## Filtering Signals

You can narrow down the signal list using filters at the top of the page.

> **Note:** Signal filtering is available on **Pro and Premium** plans.

**Available filters:**

- **Symbol** — Type a ticker to show only signals for that asset (e.g. `BTC`, `VNM`)
- **Side** — Filter to show only `BUY` signals or only `SELL` signals
- **Signal Type** — Filter by `open`, `close`, `increase`, or `decrease`
- **Date range** — Show signals from a specific time window

Click **Apply** after selecting your filters, or **Clear** to reset.

---

## Pagination

Signals are shown **15 per page**, newest first. Use the **Previous** and **Next** buttons at the bottom of the table to move between pages.

The total signal count is shown above the table so you always know how many signals match your current filter.

---

## On Mobile

On smaller screens, each signal is shown as a card rather than a table row. The left border colour tells you the direction:

- **Green border** — BUY signal
- **Red border** — SELL signal

## How to Use Signals in Practice

### For beginners

1. Open the Signals page each morning
2. Filter by your most-watched symbol
3. Look for a fresh **Open** signal with quantity above 10%
4. Check the Analysis page for that symbol to confirm the trend direction
5. If the trend and the signal agree — investigate further before trading

### For active traders

1. Keep the Signals page open in a browser tab
2. Set a filter for your watchlist symbols
3. Watch for **Increase** signals on positions you already hold — these can confirm you should stay in
4. Watch for **Close** or **Decrease** signals as exits indicators
