# Positions & Portfolio

Two of ALumiEye's most powerful features for active traders: **Positions** for tracking every open and closed trade in real time, and **Portfolio** for a visual overview of how your capital is spread.

---

## Positions Page

The Positions page shows every trade linked to your account — open, recently closed, or in transition — with live data so you always know where you stand.

### Status Types

Positions move through a lifecycle. Understanding the status helps you know what action (if any) is needed:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Opening** | A buy order has been placed and is waiting to fill |
| **Opened** | The order filled — you are now in the trade |
| **Running** | Active position with live P&L updating |
| **Closing** | A sell/exit order has been placed and is waiting to fill |
| **Cancelling** | An order is in the process of being cancelled |
| **Closed** | The trade is fully exited |

### Position Term

Each position is classified by time horizon:

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Short-term** | A trade expected to last days |
| **Mid-term** | A trade expected to last weeks |

### Reading Each Position Row

| Column | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Symbol** | The asset being traded |
| **Side** | BUY (long) or SELL (short) |
| **Status** | Current lifecycle stage (see above) |
| **Term** | Short-term or mid-term |
| **Avg Price** | Your average entry price |
| **Stop Loss** | The price at which the position will be cut to limit loss |
| **Size** | Position size as a normalised percentage of total capital |
| **Capacity** | A progress bar showing how much of the planned position size has been filled |
| **Current Price** | The latest market price (click Refresh to update) |
| **Current P&L** | Your profit or loss percentage from entry to now |
| **Position Return** | The total return percentage on the full position size |
| **Distance to Stop** | How far the current price is from your stop loss, as a percentage |

---

### Filtering Positions

Use the filter bar at the top of the page to find specific positions quickly:

- **Symbol search** — Type any ticker to filter to that asset
- **Status pills** — Click All, Running, Opening, Opened, Closing, Cancelling, or Closed
- **Active only toggle** — Check this box to hide all closed positions and show only live trades

Filters reset the page back to page 1 automatically.

---

### Refreshing Live Data

The **Current Price**, **P&L**, and **Return** columns do not update automatically to avoid overwhelming your connection. Click the **Refresh** icon on any row to pull the latest price for that specific position.

---

### Position Detail Page

Click any row to open the full detail page for that position. Here you will find:

- A summary card with all key metrics
- **Order history** — every order that made up this position (entry, partial closes, full close)
- A refresh button for live price

## Portfolio Page

> Portfolio is available on **Premium** plans only.

The Portfolio page gives you a bird's-eye view of your entire capital allocation — not just individual trades, but how everything fits together.

### Summary Cards

At the top of the page:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Total Value** | The combined value of all positions plus cash |
| **Cash** | Your available cash, with its percentage of total portfolio |
| **Active Positions** | How many positions are currently open |

### Allocation Chart

The main feature is an interactive allocation chart showing every holding as a slice:

- Asset symbol and weight percentage
- Entry price vs current price
- Return percentage (green for profit, red for loss)

Click any slice to jump directly to that position's detail page.

### Auto-Refresh

The portfolio refreshes automatically every **30 seconds**. You can also click the manual **Refresh** button to force an immediate update.

---

## How to Use Positions and Portfolio Together

**Daily routine example:**

1. Open **Positions** — check all running trades. Refresh prices for any position near its stop loss
2. Open **Portfolio** — check your allocation. If one asset dominates the chart, consider whether you are over-exposed
3. Cross-reference with **Signals** — if you see a Close signal for an asset you hold, check the position's current P&L and decide whether to act
